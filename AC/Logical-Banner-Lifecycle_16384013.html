Introduction

This section describes the logical lifecycle of a student on Banner in the context of their network account.

Student States

The following state diagram describes the possible student states in Banner, as far as an external account management application is concerned.

Each of the above states is derived from one or more Banner stages.

Accepted

When an offer against an application for study, made either through U.C.A.S. or Ascent One, is firmly accepted by the applicant then the student is considered to be Accepted. This can be summarised as the student being likely to study at the university, but not yet doing so.

Details of Banner Data Changes

An application choice record, as held on the baseline table SKRUCCR, has the value 'A' recorded in the column SKRUCCR_SSDT_CODE_AP_RESP. The record should meet the following additional criteria:

  • The proposed year of entry, as held in SKRUCCR_PROP_ENTRY_YR, is for a current or future year.
  • The proposed month of entry, as held in SKRUCCR_PROP_ENTRY_MTH, is for a current or future month. This must be determined in conjunction with value in SKRUCCR_PROP_ENTRY_YR.

Studying

When an accepted student registers via Online Registration, they become Studying. This means that they are now studying at the university and will require access to facilities appropriate to their course. They remain in this state through subsequent registrations in the following years, depending upon the length of their programme of study.

Details of Banner Data Changes

The student has a record for a current term, as identified by a corresponding STVTERM record that bounds the current date, in the registration table SFBETRM. The student transitions to a Studying state when the registration status, as identified by the column SFBETRM_ESTS_CODE is changed to either 'RE', 'RP' or 'RS'. This usually occurs during online registration and is identified in the above state diagram as [registration]. It will also occur when a student transitions from an Interrupted state, shown above as [reinstatement].

Note that when a SFBETRM record is created for a subsequent term, it will initially be at status 'EL'. This does not cause a state transition from Studying. Also note that a student can have a registration status of 'WU', which means 'writing up'. For the purposes of account management, this will also not trigger a transition from Studying, as a 'writing up' student will still require access to university facilities.

Interrupted

When a studying student needs to suspend their study on a temporary basis, they become Interrupted. Note that this is a temporary status and that the student can only transition to and from a Studying state.

Details of Banner Data Changes

The student has a record for a current term, as identified by a corresponding STVTERM record that bounds the current date, in the registration table SFBETRM. The student transitions to an Interrupted state when the registration status, as identified by the column SFBETRM_ESTS_CODE is changed to either 'IN', or 'SU'. This is shown on the state diagram as [interruption].

Left

When a student leaves their course without successfully completing it, they become Left. Note that if a Left student subsequently returns to the university, they must start the admissions process afresh and may attain an initial state of Accepted.

Details of Banner Data Changes

The student has a record for a current term, as identified by a corresponding STVTERM record that bounds the current date, in the registration table SFBETRM. The student transitions to a Left state when the registration status, as identified by the column SFBETRM_ESTS_CODE is changed to either 'RR', 'WD' or 'WN'. This is shown on the state digram as [withdrawal].

Note About Left Students

If a student who has previously left without successfully completing their course and then re-applies for a study place, they will remain at state Left until they receive a firm acceptance of their application, when they become Accepted again.

Graduated

This Graduated state is reached when a student successfully completes their programme of study. A Graduated student will retain this state if they subsequently return to study another programme; see the Note About Graduates below.

Details of Banner Data Changes

When a student graduates, their registration record - SFBETRM - does not undergo a status change. Instead, the Graduated state transition is triggered by the graduation status, as recorded in SHRDGMR_DEGS_CODE, for the degree record (SHRDGMR) of the current programme of study becoming 'AW' (i.e. 'awarded'). This is shown on the state diagram as [graduation].

Note About Graduates

When a student graduates and attains the Graduated state, they will retain this state in addition to state derived from their stage in Banner, if they subsequently return to the university. Putting this simply, a returning graduate, if accepted for study on a further course, will attain an initial state of Accepted+Graduated. After registration, their status will become Studying+Graduated, and so on. They do not and will not lose their Graduated state.
