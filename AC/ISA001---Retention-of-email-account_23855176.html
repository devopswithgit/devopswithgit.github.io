Accman : ISA001 - Retention of email account

Created by Jeremy Sugden, last modified on Dec 24, 2015

User Story

Prefix
Detail
As a <Actor>Student
I want <Need>to have access to my university email account while I am on an interruption of study
So that <Reason>I can retain access to historic emails both sent and received by me and allow me to keep my contact and distribution lists for the University.
JIRA 
MoSCow PriorityM

Supporting Context

The student may need to refer to historic emails relating to their programme of study, correspondence with university staff and fellow students and student groups. The email account should not be disabled when the student starts their interruption.   Currently there are a set of processes that run several times a day, triggered by changes in student statuses in the Banner student record system.  These processes, which automatically disable Interrupted student accounts, will be inactivated. 

There is also a  a web interface, which enables certain users, (such as Support, or Library, staff), to click a button to manually disable an account.  Text will be displayed next to the button for interrupting students, reminding the user that Interrupting students are now entitled to retain their account.  

If the user continues to click the Disable button, a pop up message box is displayed informing them that the account should not normally be disabled, and asking them to confirm this decision.If they choose to continue, then the Office 365 account will be disabled.  This is to cover cases where for example the student has breached the University's regulations.
