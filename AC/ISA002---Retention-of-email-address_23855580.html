Accman : ISA002 - Retention of email address

Created by Jeremy Sugden, last modified on Dec 24, 2015

User Story

Prefix
Detail
As a <Actor>member of staff at the university
I want <Need>the student to have access to their university email account whilst they are interrupted
So that <Reason>I can retain contact the student, and improve the student's feeling of inclusion.
JIRA 
MoSCow PriorityM

Supporting Context

The email address should be in the same format as the active student account, so the student will notice no change.  The account will continue to be displayed in email directories and on distribution lists. Currently when an Office 365 account is disabled, AccMan also hides the student in the Exchange and Office 365 address books as a separate process, this will be changed to prevent interrupted students from being hidden in these address books.

Jeremy - Esme Caulfied has offered the following response on this matter.  I hope this resolves the matter. Annette

I can’t think of any reasons as to why interrupted students shouldn’t receive all-student emails.  The main thing would be to ensure that banner data is up to date as our list of students who receive student Newsletters and all-student emails comes from Banner and is then exported into Mailchimp, which we use to send all-student emails.  We’d just need to ensure that there was a process in place for ensuring that our student details were correct and we were emailing the right population.

The 2 main types of emails we send to all students are the Student Newsletters which are a summary of key news stories and events – think it’s a really good idea to send these to interrupting students as may help them feel connected with the University.  We also send occasional all student email bulletins about matters such as building closures, which may be less relevant.

Generally, I think that sending the Student Newsletter to interrupting students is a really good idea, however the email bulletins should be considered on a case by case basis as not all may be relevant to interrupting students.
