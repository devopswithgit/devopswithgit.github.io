Introduction

Staff and people on fixed term contracts follow a slightly different lifecycle to Associates. SAP initiates the process. A member of HR staff inputs their details into SAP, and then they manually send an email to the new starter's manager. The email instructs them to go to the Staff Self Activation website. The email contains their personnel number and looks like the example below.

Email to Manager

Dear <Manager>

Please see below details relating to your new starter and the various steps that need to be taken as part of her induction with the University.

Step 1: Payroll number (please note this is the sometimes referred to as a Personnel number)

Please accept this email as confirmation that Harry is now set up on SAP and  her  payroll number is 123456.

Step 2: IT Account

She will now need an IT account set up. Please click the following link ‘register for your University network account’ which it will take you through to the relevant IT web page. In the event you experience any technical issues please contact IT Services on x. 52444.

Step 3: ID Card

As a result of setting the IT account up information relating to the individual is sent through to Estates which allows for ID cards to be issued. It is recommended you ring x.54444 to check the details have been received by Estates, before going to Maxwell, as they are unable to issue ID cards without this. The Single ID Card Use policy can be found by clicking the following link http://www.salford.ac.uk/__data/assets/pdf_file/0003/195132/SingleIDCardUsePolicyV1.1.pdf

Code of practice

In addition to the above, please click the following link to the Code of Practice for the Induction of staff http://www.hr.salford.ac.uk/policies-procedures/?letter=I. Please familiarise yourself with this with reference to the employee, particularly with regards to your roles and responsibilities as line manager as stated on pages 4 and 5. 

Probationary period

The probationary period is for 12 months or for the duration of her appointment, whichever is longer, and you will be sent the interim probation memo 4 months after her start date and the final probation at 8 months.  These will be sent to you electronically and you must follow the procedure for the review of staff on probation, please click the following link http://www.hr.salford.ac.uk/policies-procedures/?letter=P. Please complete and return the memos to Human Resources. 

DSE Self-Assessment    

All new employees are required to complete a DSE online self-assessment upon commencement of employment.  Please ensure your new starter is aware of this on their first day; and ask them to fill in the online form through the Safety website.

Should you have any queries, please feel free to contact HR Central Services.

Kind regards

-A member of HR staff

When the member of staff arrives, they use their managers PC to go to the Accman Staff Self Activation site and enter their personnel number, surname and date of birth. Providing a PERSON record matching these details exists in the Accman database (and the status is not LEFT), Accman will retrieve the next available pregenerated account for their department, and link it to the PERSON record via a REGISTER transaction. This is how an Accman ACCOUNT record is allocated to a member of staff. The ACCOUNT record holds the username (e.g. UIS123) and the self activation website presents the username to the new starter on the final screen.

This also begins the process of setting up their AD, Sateon and Exchange accounts. It takes around 90 minutes for this to complete, mainly because their is a forced delay to allow for AD replication to take place. The manager sometimes performs this step on behalf of the new starter, usually to avoid the delay new starters typically experience when they first arrive which can be frustrating.

In summary, at the point when a new starter's details have been imported from SAP, but before they have accessed the staff self activation website as per the instructions in the email, the user's account will be as follows

  • A PERSON record will exist
  • The PERSON record will be in one of the following states
    • Present if the SAP status is A and the start date is not in the future
    • Expected if the SAP status is A and the start date is in the future
    • Left if the SAP status is W
  • There will not be an ACCOUNT record associated with the PERSON record yet
  • There will be no AD, Sateon (ID card), Exchange (email) accounts set up yet. This is why they have to use a manager's PC to activate their account - they are unable to log into the network themselves until they have an AD account.

Staff Self Activation

The staff self activation screens are shown below. Providing the PERSON record's status is not LEFT (PRESENT/EXPECTED will work fine), and they enter the correct details, a new ACCOUNT will be allocated to the person. The username and the pre-generated password associated with that account is displayed on the screen. It also sends an email to service desk to inform them that a new member of staff is having their account set up. 

Changing from EXPECTED to PRESENT

There is some confusion around the question of when does a member of staff's PERSON.PERSON_STATE changes from EXPECTED to PRESENT. Here's the logic that the SAP Import uses each time it runs:

  • Any member of staff on the SAP Import (which runs twice a day) who is marked 'A' (Active) will by default, be initially treated as PRESENT. Unless the next point is true, their status will be updated to PRESENT in the Accman database. This is also true, even if they are already PRESENT. They will simply remain PRESENT.
  • If their start date is in the future however, that status will be changed to EXPECTED, and the Accman database updated accordingly.
  • New staff may not be able to log in immediately on arriving at the university for the first time. They may have been activated previously, but they will still be EXPECTED in Accman. A SAP Import needs to run after their start date becomes effective, and unfortunately the first SAP Import of the day runs at around 13:10. When this runs, Accman will see that they are now here (SAP will have put an Active record in the sap_import table by then) and the Staff AutoState process (part of the SAP import) will update them to PRESENT.

Comments:

Some notes:

During the SAP Import, the following SAP statuses are converted to Accman equivalents

A => Present

W => Left

The Accman status can be overridden later on in the process if their start date is in the future. If this is the case, they will be marked as Expected instead. I don’t think this will happen if SAP overrides their original start date with an ‘early’ start date.

When they self-activate, Accman checks that a person record exists with the personnel number, date of birth and surname that they enter. Their person record must also not have a status of ‘LEFT’. If these things are all true, it will pick the next available username (based on their department) and start setting up the account (creating AD logins etc).
