Accman : Person/Account Status vs. Alumnus Status in AccMan

Created by Julia Kelbrick on Aug 09, 2012

We had an incident (Support Case 254814 d/d. 06/08/2012) were a Library Enquiry Desk Specialist was interpreting the Alumnus Status of a student as implying that the student's network account was disabled.  The following email explains the difference between Person / Account Status in AccMan and their "is_alumnus" status . . . .

 

Hi Roy,

Looking in AccMan, the account status for student PYP865 is PRESENT / VALID, therefore, as I understand it they should still be able to log in to the network and be able to use the printing facilities.

At the point when the student has officially left the University their AccMan status will change to LEFT / DISABLED.  At this time they will neither be able to log in to the network nor will they be able to log in to the On-line Printer Credits System.  Consequently they should never be in a position where they can make a mistake and pay in to an account that will no longer support printing.

As I understand it, schools can sometimes be late in changing some student statuses to ‘LEFT’, which may be the reason this student can still log in to the network.

It is important to note though that the students “Alumnus” status in AccMan is something very different from the “Account Status” mentioned above.

Students are flagged as “Alumni” after they have graduated.  This simply acts as a flag to AccMan to say that when their network account is eventually disabled, then their Live@Edu email account will not also be disabled.  This is so we can provide graduates with “Email for Life”.

I hope this answers your query and please let me know if there is anything that I have misunderstood.

Kind regards,

Julia.
